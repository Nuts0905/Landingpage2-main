AC Milan, officially known as Associazione Calcio Milan, is one of the most historic and prestigious football clubs in Italy and the world. Founded on December 16, 1899, by Englishman Alfred Edwards, this Milanese club has been a symbol of sport in Italy for over a century. Since its early days, the team has sported the characteristic red and black colors, representing the fire of its players and the intensity of their competitive spirit. Its stadium, the famous San Siro, is one of the temples of European football, hosting fans from around the world at every match.

AC Milan has earned an esteemed position in European and international football thanks to its impressive trophy cabinet. With 19 Serie A titles, it has been one of the most dominant teams in the Italian league. Additionally, in European competitions, Milan has won seven European Cups/UEFA Champions League titles, a record matched or surpassed by only a few teams. It has also claimed international titles, such as the Intercontinental Cup and the Club World Cup, establishing itself as one of the most successful clubs in football history.

The Milan derby, known as the "Derbi della Madonnina," is one of the most exciting and anticipated matchups in world football. This clash pits AC Milan against their eternal rivals, Inter Milan, in an atmosphere charged with passion and rivalry. Held in the iconic San Siro stadium, the derby attracts thousands of fans and creates an electrifying ambiance that resonates throughout the city. The rivalry between the two clubs goes beyond football; it represents the identity and pride of two factions within the same city. Over the years, the derby has witnessed memorable moments, spectacular goals, and intense competition that continue to captivate fans of both teams.
